Website Accessibility

At its core, website accessibility ensures everyone is able to effectively use and interact with online content. By making websites accessible, we are able to create inclusive digital environemnts where everyone is able to access necessary information, products, and services.

Aside from being good practice, accessibility is also a legal requirement in many different countries. For instance, in the United States, the Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA) requires digital spaces to be accessible to those with disabilities, similar to how physical public spaces must accomodate accessibility needs. The ADA requires businesses, Educational institutions, and governemnt websites to follow certain guidelines and present accessible content.

To guide website creators, the Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG), created by the World Wide Web Consortium (W3C) were made to outline best practices for creating accessible websites. The WCAG focuses on four key concepts, they are as follows:

  • Perceivable: Content must be presented in ways users can perceive, for example providing alternative text for images.
  • Operable: All users should be able to effectively interact with and navigate the website, regardless of any assistive technology used.
  • Understandable: Information and operation of the User Interface should be clear and easy to comprehend.
  • Robust: Content should be adaptable with different assistive technologies.

Adhering to both the ADA and WCAG standards, we can guarantee our websites are inclusive and legally complient, preventing discrimination and promoting equal access.

Copyright and Fair Use

Answer Key!

  1. Question One - Fair Use is a legal doctrine that allows limited use of copyrighted material without requiring permission from the rights holders. It enables educators, researchers, and other creators to use portions of copyrighted works for purposes such as criticism, comment, news reporting, teaching, scholarship, or research, provided that such use meets certain criteria.

  2. Question Two- Purpose and character of use, nature of the copyrighted work, amount and substantiality, and effect on the market.

  3. Question Three - Fair use benefits instructional designers by allowing them to incorporate various copyrighted materials—such as texts, images, videos, and music—into their course materials without needing explicit permission. This enables them to create richer, more engaging educational experiences, support critical thinking, and foster discussions while promoting diverse viewpoints.

  4. Question Four - Creative Commons is a nonprofit organization that provides free licenses and public domain tools for creators to share their works legally. Instructional designers can use Creative Commons-licensed materials to enhance their course content by remixing, adapting, and incorporating these resources into their materials, thus saving time and fostering collaboration.

  5. Question Five - A CC BY license (Attribution) allows others to use, distribute, and adapt the work for any purpose, including commercial use, as long as the original creator is credited. In contrast, a CC BY-NC license (Attribution-NonCommercial) permits use and adaptation of the work but only for non-commercial purposes, requiring the original creator to be credited as well.

  6. Question Six - The ShareAlike (SA) condition requires that if instructional designers adapt or remix a Creative Commons-licensed work with the ShareAlike condition, they must distribute their resulting work under the same or a compatible license. This ensures that adaptations remain accessible under similar terms, promoting continued sharing and collaboration.

  7. Question Seven - The key difference between "Attribution" (CC BY) and "NonCommercial" (CC BY-NC) licenses lies in the permission for commercial use. The CC BY license allows for both commercial and non-commercial use as long as credit is given to the original creator, making it more flexible for instructional designers who might sell their materials. In contrast, the CC BY-NC license restricts use to non-commercial purposes only, which could limit the ability to use the content in paid courses or materials.

  8. Question Eight - Public domain resources are works that are no longer under copyright protection, meaning they can be freely used, modified, and distributed without permission or payment. Instructional designers can use public domain resources to create educational materials, enhancing their content with historical texts, images, and other resources without legal restrictions.

  9. Question Nine - Any two examples you find are perfect!

  10. Question Ten - Copyright Status: Public domain resources are not protected by copyright and can be used freely without restriction, while Creative Commons resources are still under copyright but offer specific permissions for use and adaptation based on the chosen license. Usage Rights: Public domain works can be used for any purpose without crediting the original creator, whereas Creative Commons resources typically require attribution to the creator and may have additional conditions (such as non-commercial use or share-alike).

Use this answer key more as a guideline! A lot of the questions were open ended and creating and sharing your opinions is valid!
